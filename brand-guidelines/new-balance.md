# New Balance — Brand & Creative Guidelines

## Brand Identity
New Balance is a heritage athletic brand rooted in craftsmanship, authenticity, and understated confidence. Unlike flashy competitors, NB speaks to people who value quality over hype — from serious runners to streetwear enthusiasts.

## Voice & Tone
- Warm, authentic, confident without boasting
- Speaks to the individual, not the crowd
- Storytelling-adjacent — a moment, a feeling, a place
- Never try-hard or trend-chasing
- Headlines: conversational, 4–8 words, often lowercase or sentence case

## Color Palette
- Primary: Cardinal Red (#CF3A2C) and Grey (#6B7280)
- Secondary: Off-white (#F5F0EB), warm charcoal (#2D2D2D)
- Accent: use red sparingly for CTAs
- Avoid neon, electric, or overly saturated tones

## Typography Direction
- Clean but warm sans-serif
- Sentence case preferred over ALL CAPS
- White text on dark backgrounds, dark on light

## Photography & Image Style
- Urban lifestyle: streets, sidewalks, coffee shops, parks at golden hour
- Natural light preferred — warm, slightly moody
- Subjects: real people, authentic moments, not posed athletes
- Texture: concrete, brick, weathered surfaces
- Warm earth tones and film-like quality
- Products in context — worn, lived-in, not on pedestals
- Heritage feel: think aged quality, not newness

## Ad Copy Direction
- Headline: a feeling or a place connected to the query context (e.g. "Made for the everyday mile.", "Streets were always the point.")
- Body: 1 sentence about the product's craft or fit for the user's lifestyle
- CTA: approachable action ("Shop the look", "Find your pair", "Explore styles")

## What to Avoid
- Aggressive/loud language
- High-energy action photography (that's Nike's territory)
- Emojis
- Corporate tone
- Overly minimal — warmth matters

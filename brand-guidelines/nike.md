# Nike — Brand & Creative Guidelines

## Brand Identity
Nike stands for athletic performance, motivation, and human potential. Every piece of communication should feel bold, direct, and inspiring. The brand challenges people to push their limits.

## Voice & Tone
- Bold and direct — short punchy sentences
- Motivational without being preachy
- First-person or imperative ("Run further", "Just do it", "You've got this")
- Never corporate or stiff
- Headlines: 3–6 words max, high impact

## Color Palette
- Primary: Black (#111111) and White (#FFFFFF)
- Accent: Electric Red (#E31837) for CTAs and emphasis
- Never use pastels or muted tones in primary placements

## Typography Direction
- Condensed, bold sans-serif
- Headlines ALL CAPS or sentence case — never mixed
- High contrast against background

## Photography & Image Style
- High-contrast dramatic lighting, dark backgrounds preferred
- Action-oriented: motion blur, dynamic angles, athletes mid-movement
- Minimalist compositions — one subject, strong negative space
- Black/white photography with a single color accent works well
- Surfaces: tracks, concrete, gym floors — urban athletic environments
- No clutter, no product stacks — one hero moment
- Photorealistic, cinematic quality

## Ad Copy Direction
- Headline: short battle cry tied to the specific context (e.g. "Own the race.", "Built for this moment.")
- Body: 1 sentence connecting the product to the user's specific need/query
- CTA: action verb + destination ("Shop runners", "Find your fit", "Get training gear")

## What to Avoid
- Passive language
- Long body copy
- Cluttered visuals
- Emojis
- Generic "buy now" CTAs
